Mr. Dennis Ritchie

A great contributor for CS

  • 1941 - born in Bronxville, New York.
  • 1967 - began working at Bell Labs Computing Sciences Research Center.
  • 1968 - he defended his PhD thesis on "Program Structure and Computational Complexity" at Harvard, but he never got his Doctor's degree
  • In the 1960s, he worked on Multics with Ken Thompson. However, the project was killed by 1969
  • He was also involved with the development of two other operating systems (Inferno and Plan 9) and of the Limbo programming language
  • Thanks to his work, he received many awards, such as Turing Award (in 1983), National Medal of Technology (1998), IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal (1990), Computer History Museum Fellow (1997), Harold Pender Award (2003) and Japan Prize (2011)
  • He died in 2011, in Berkeley Heights, New Jersey, even though his legacy to Computer Science remains intrinsic to all the Technology we have today.
